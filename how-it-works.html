How the Truck ETA Calculator Works

The Truck ETA Calculator is designed to estimate the arrival time of a truck based on several factors including distance, average speed, start time, and hours left to drive before starting. Here's how it works:

  • Distance: The total distance to be traveled in miles.
  • Speed: The average speed of the truck in miles per hour.
  • Start Time: The time when the trip is scheduled to start.
  • Hours Left: The number of hours the driver has left to drive before starting the trip.

The calculator takes into account the following constraints:

  • Driving Hours Per Day: Maximum of 11 hours of driving per day.
  • Break Time: A mandatory 30-minute break after 8 hours of driving.
  • Off-Duty Time: 10 hours of rest after a driving shift.
  • Pre-Trip and Post-Trip Inspections: 15 minutes each for inspections.
  • Fueling Time: 30 minutes for refueling.

By considering these factors, the calculator provides an estimated time of arrival (ETA) in ISO format.
